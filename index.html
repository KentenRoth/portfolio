About Me

Hello, My name is Kent Roth. I am currently an automotive technician, but I am looking to make the transition to a developer role. My current skills include HTML, CSS, and I am currently learning Javascript. I have been thinking about changing careers for a few years, and now I have started to teach myself how to code with courses on Udemy.com and Devslopes.com. I make time each night to do something that will help me advance towards the goal of becoming a developer. The thing that keeps me so interested in coding is the constant challenges and the amount of problem-solving that goes along with it. I also enjoy how many options of different languages and frameworks there are that you can learn. Coding was something that was very interesting to me in high school, but at that time my school only provided basic HTML and CSS class that didn't even last an entire semester. After high school, I enrolled at Hamilton College to learn computer science but was soon overwhelmed by how much it costs to live on my own. Due to financial issues, I had to drop out of college and go full time working. I started working as an automotive technician, and I have been with the same company for the last 14 years. During those 14 years, I got a lot better with managing my finances and enrolled at Kaplan University (now Purdue Global) and earned my Bachelor's Degree in Business Administration.

If I'm not working or coding I like to have family time, golf, play video games, and read. I started golfing a few years ago, and even though I am not very good, I find it very relaxing. I think its just the being outside having fun with friends that lets the stress melt away. When it comes to video games, I love booting up my old NES and playing Tecmo Super Bowl If I am playing on a current generation console I try to play some Elder Scrolls Online, but I am looking forward to Battlefield 5, and Fallout 76. Reading has been a new found joy over the last few years. I use to get very tired very quick with reading, but over the last few years I have been reading a lot more, and I find it very enjoyable. I do not have a favorite genre of books. I like to mix it up to give myself a wider variety of things. I am currently reading The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho.
